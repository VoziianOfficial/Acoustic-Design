Planning Scope

What the Direction Covers

The planning process organizes the room information into practical priorities rather than presenting a generic shopping list.

Room Layout

Review dimensions, proportions, openings, major furniture, equipment, and movement through the space.

Priority Zones

Identify likely listening, microphone, side-wall, rear-wall, ceiling, corner, door, and window priorities.

Treatment Direction

Explore suitable panel depth, placement, air gaps, movable options, ceiling treatment, and boundary control.

Material Context

Compare appearance, depth, backing, installation, maintenance, and verified product information.

Isolation Expectations

Separate internal room treatment from transmission control involving gaps, mass, structure, and flanking paths.

Next Steps

Organize practical changes and identify where qualified product, installation, or building-specific input may be appropriate.

Before Submitting

Prepare the Room

Perfect drawings are not required. Clear measurements, a simple sketch, photographs, and an honest description of the problem are more useful than polished but incomplete information.

Go to the request form

Measure the Main Dimensions

Record the internal length, width, and height. Note alcoves, sloped ceilings, open doorways, and connected areas that change the room volume.

Photograph Each Direction

Capture the front, rear, left, right, ceiling, floor, doors, windows, desk, equipment, seating, and current treatment where possible.

Sketch the Current Layout

Mark the approximate positions of furniture, listening seat, desk, speakers, microphones, instruments, doors, and windows.

Describe What You Hear

Explain where the issue is most noticeable, what activity reveals it, whether it changes around the room, and what has already been tried.

Define the Visual Direction

Share preferred colours, materials, shapes, visible finishes, discreet treatment goals, and any interior references relevant to the room.

Set Realistic Limits

Include the available budget range, rental restrictions, mounting limits, construction permissions, timeline, and whether specialist installation is possible.

Planning Notes

Useful Details to Share

These prompts help describe the room more clearly, especially when the main concern is difficult to name.

01

Voice and Microphone Use

Describe where the speaker sits or stands, microphone type if known, typical speaking distance, and whether the room is used for calls, podcasts, streaming, or voice recording.

02

Listening and Speakers

Share speaker size, approximate distance from walls, listening distance, desk dimensions, and whether the room is mainly for mixing, music listening, editing, or entertainment.

03

Bass Behaviour

Explain whether bass feels stronger near walls or corners, weak at the listening seat, uneven across the room, or difficult to judge at different volumes.

04

Transmission Concern

Describe the sound source, receiving room, time of day, walls or floors involved, doors, windows, ventilation, and whether the issue is airborne sound or impact noise.

Important Context

What a Plan Cannot Promise

Acoustic outcomes depend on the actual room, construction, products, placement, workmanship, and how the completed space is used.

Total Silence

No responsible planning page should promise complete silence or perfect isolation without building-specific assessment and suitable construction.

Guaranteed Performance

Results cannot be guaranteed from room descriptions alone, especially without measurements, inspection, verified product data, or installation review.

Automatic Installation

Submitting an inquiry does not confirm installation, construction, product supply, pricing, scheduling, or provider availability.

Building Approval

Structural, electrical, ventilation, moisture, fire-rated, and landlord-related requirements remain separate project considerations.
