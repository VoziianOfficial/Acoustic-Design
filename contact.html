Useful Context

What to Include

A few specific details can make an acoustic inquiry much easier to understand.

Room Dimensions

Length, width, height, alcoves, open areas, sloped ceilings, and any unusual geometry.

Current Layout

Doors, windows, desk, speakers, microphone, seating, major furniture, storage, and existing treatment.

Main Sound Concern

Echo, speech clarity, recording, monitoring, bass, panel placement, airborne sound, impact noise, or leakage.

Visual Priorities

Preferred colours, materials, shapes, visible finishes, discreet treatment, and relevant interior references.

Practical Limits

Budget context, rental restrictions, mounting limits, deadlines, construction permissions, and available depth.

Location Context

Country or broad project region where relevant to availability, building practices, or possible specialist routing.
