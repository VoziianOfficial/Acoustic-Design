Voice First

Build Around the Microphone Zone

A clear streaming setup begins with the relationship between the speaker, microphone, desk, nearby boundaries, equipment, and visible background.

A room used for live streaming may also serve as a bedroom, office, editing space, gaming room, podcast area, or meeting environment. The acoustic direction should respect those shared uses.

Before selecting panels, review microphone type, speaking distance, gain, desk size, monitor placement, computer noise, windows, doors, ceiling height, camera framing, lighting, and available treatment surfaces.

Streaming Treatment Direction

Control the Voice Environment

Each change should support clearer speech, lower distraction, comfortable operation, and a camera-ready room without confusing internal treatment with structural sound isolation.

01

Microphone Position

Closer microphone placement can reduce the relative level of room reflections, but the setup should remain comfortable, visually suitable, and compatible with the microphone’s design.

  • Review speaking distance and microphone gain together.
  • Use the polar pattern to reject avoidable noise where possible.
  • Control plosives, vibration, keyboard noise, and boom-arm movement.
02

Nearby Wall Treatment

Walls close to the speaker and microphone may create strong short-delay reflections. Suitable treatment zones depend on orientation, distance, panel depth, coverage, and the rest of the room.

  • Review the wall behind and beside the speaking position.
  • Consider movable panels where the room is shared.
  • Balance visible design with actual microphone reflection paths.
03

Desk and Screens

The desk, monitors, lighting equipment, control surfaces, and accessories create nearby reflection and noise sources that should be included in the room plan.

  • Review microphone height relative to the desk surface.
  • Reduce unnecessary reflective clutter near the microphone.
  • Keep fans, consoles, and mechanical devices as quiet as practical.
04

Camera Background

Visible treatment can contribute to both acoustic control and visual identity, but its location should still relate to the microphone, voice path, and room behaviour.

  • Coordinate panel scale with the camera crop and lighting.
  • Avoid reflective decorative surfaces close to the voice zone.
  • Use colour, texture, depth, and asymmetry intentionally.
05

Equipment and External Noise

Computer fans, air systems, keyboards, chairs, doors, windows, household activity, and street sound may become noticeable once room reflections are reduced.

  • Move noisy equipment away from the microphone where practical.
  • Use quieter operating modes and vibration control where suitable.
  • Separate noise reduction from structural soundproofing expectations.
Setup Comparisons

Test the Arrangement

Small changes to microphone distance, desk orientation, visible treatment, and equipment location can improve a streaming setup before more permanent work is considered.

Setup 01

Move the Microphone Closer

Increase the direct voice level before increasing microphone gain or relying only on wall treatment.

Setup 02

Change the Orientation

Rotate the desk or speaking position to reduce strong nearby reflections, window glare, or direct noise exposure where possible.

Setup 03

Integrate the Background

Coordinate treatment, lighting, shelving, colour, and camera depth while keeping the microphone environment functional.

Setup 04

Use Movable Treatment

Freestanding or reversible treatment can support shared bedrooms, rented rooms, changing camera angles, and temporary recording setups.

Planning Information

Prepare the Streaming Details

Useful room and equipment information makes it easier to separate microphone, reflection, equipment-noise, visual, and sound-transmission concerns.

Request a Streaming Plan
01

Measure the Room

Record length, width, height, doors, windows, alcoves, ceiling details, and any connected open areas.

02

Map the Workstation

Show the desk, chair, microphone, screens, computer, camera, lights, speakers, doors, windows, furniture, and existing panels.

03

Describe the Microphone

Include microphone type if known, polar pattern, boom arm, speaking distance, gain, and typical voice position.

04

Name the Main Problem

Explain whether the concern involves echo, hollow voice, keyboard noise, computer fans, street noise, household sound, or panel placement.

05

Share the Camera View

Provide the camera angle, crop, background area, lighting position, preferred colours, finishes, and visible treatment direction.

06

Define the Limits

Include budget context, rental restrictions, mounting rules, shared-room needs, available depth, deadlines, and installation limits.
