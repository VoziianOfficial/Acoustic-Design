Cookie Policy

Overview

Echoform uses limited browser-storage technologies to support website operation, security, accessibility, form handling, and preference retention.

Optional analytics, advertising, personalization, or third-party technologies should be activated only where they are configured, permitted by applicable law, and consistent with the consent choice shown to the visitor.

Current Default Approach

The standard Echoform website configuration uses local browser storage to remember whether you accepted or declined the cookie notice. Essential website functions remain available regardless of that choice.

What Cookies and Browser Storage Are

Cookies

Cookies are small text records that a website or related service can place on a browser or device. They may contain an identifier, preference, timestamp, or other limited information.

Local Storage

Local storage is a browser feature that allows a website to save limited information on a device without sending that information with every page request.

Session Storage

Session storage is similar to local storage but is normally cleared when the relevant browser tab or session ends.

Similar Technologies

Websites may also use pixels, tags, scripts, software-development tools, embedded content, server logs, and device or browser signals for functions similar to cookies.

Technologies We May Use

Depending on the active website configuration, Echoform may use:

  • First-party cookies created directly by the Echoform website.
  • Local storage used to remember the cookie-consent decision.
  • Session storage used for temporary page or form state.
  • Server logs used for security, technical operation, troubleshooting, and abuse prevention.
  • Content-delivery or hosting technologies needed to serve website files.
  • Optional analytics technologies used to understand general website use.
  • Optional advertising or campaign-measurement technologies where explicitly enabled.
  • Third-party scripts required to provide fonts, icons, animations, sliders, or similar website components.

The presence of a third-party script does not automatically mean that the third party sets a cookie. Its behaviour depends on the provider, configuration, browser, location, and current service implementation.

Cookie and Storage Categories

Essential

Supports security, navigation, accessibility, form operation, consent storage, and functions required for the website to work.

Preferences

Remembers choices such as consent status, display preferences, or other settings where those features are enabled.

Analytics

Helps understand general page use, navigation patterns, technical performance, and website improvement opportunities.

Advertising

May support campaign measurement, audience understanding, or relevant promotion where such technologies are separately enabled.

Essential Storage

Essential technologies may operate without optional consent where permitted because they are necessary to provide the website or a function you request.

Essential purposes may include:

  • Remembering whether you accepted or declined the cookie notice.
  • Supporting website navigation and responsive menu behaviour.
  • Protecting forms against spam, duplicate submissions, abuse, and automated requests.
  • Maintaining temporary form or interface state.
  • Supporting security, load balancing, hosting, content delivery, and error handling.
  • Remembering accessibility or interface preferences where such functions are enabled.
  • Providing content or services specifically requested by the visitor.

Blocking essential browser storage may cause some features to reset, repeat, or operate less reliably.

Preference and Consent Storage

The Echoform cookie notice uses a browser-storage record to remember the choice you make.

The stored value may indicate that you:

  • Accepted the cookie notice.
  • Declined optional technologies.
  • Have not yet made a choice.

This record is saved on the browser and device where the choice was made. A different browser, private-browsing session, cleared browser history, or separate device may not share the same choice.

You can reopen the cookie controls through the footer and update your preference. Clearing the storage may cause the cookie notice to appear again.

Analytics Technologies

Analytics technologies may be used to understand how visitors generally interact with the website. Information may include:

  • Pages viewed and approximate navigation sequence.
  • General referral source.
  • Browser, operating system, device category, and language.
  • Approximate session duration and interaction patterns.
  • Technical performance, loading, and error information.
  • General geographic information derived from an Internet Protocol address.

Where consent is required, optional analytics should remain inactive until the visitor has made the necessary choice.

Analytics information should be used for website operation, content improvement, performance review, security, accessibility, and general audience understanding rather than to make decisions producing legal or similarly significant effects about an individual.

Advertising and Campaign Technologies

Echoform may provide an advertising and collaboration contact channel. This does not mean that advertising trackers are automatically active on the website.

If campaign-measurement or advertising technologies are introduced, they may be used to:

  • Measure whether a campaign directed visitors to the website.
  • Understand broad campaign performance.
  • Limit repeated advertising where supported.
  • Attribute an inquiry to a marketing source.
  • Understand aggregate audience interests.

Where applicable law requires consent, such technologies should not operate before consent is obtained.

No Automatic Advertising Tracking

The presence of an advertising or collaboration page does not by itself indicate that behavioural advertising cookies, profiling tools, or cross-site tracking are active.

Third-Party Technologies

The website may load resources from independent providers to support visual presentation and interface functions. These may include font, icon, animation, slider, hosting, or content-delivery providers.

A third party may receive technical request information such as an Internet Protocol address, browser information, requested resource, date, time, and referring page when the browser connects to that provider.

Third-party technologies are governed by the provider’s own terms and privacy practices. Echoform does not control every browser, network, caching, logging, or storage practice used by an independent provider.

Where practical, optional third-party scripts should be limited, configured conservatively, or loaded only after any consent required by law.

How Long Cookies and Storage Last

Storage duration depends on the technology and purpose.

Session Technologies

Session records normally expire when the browser tab, browser session, or temporary process ends.

Persistent Technologies

Persistent records remain until their configured expiry date, until they are replaced, until the website removes them, or until the visitor clears browser data.

Server Logs

Server, security, hosting, and technical logs may be retained separately according to operational, legal, security, and provider requirements.

Echoform aims to avoid retaining browser identifiers longer than reasonably necessary for the relevant purpose.

Your Cookie and Storage Controls

You can control cookies and browser storage in several ways.

Echoform Cookie Controls

Use the cookie-settings control in the website footer or the button below to reopen the consent interface.

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to view, block, delete, or restrict cookies and site data. The available controls vary by browser and device.

  • Block all or selected cookies.
  • Delete cookies and local storage for a specific website.
  • Clear browsing data when the browser closes.
  • Use private or incognito browsing modes.
  • Restrict third-party cookies.
  • Review site permissions and stored data.

Device and Network Controls

Some devices, privacy software, browser extensions, firewalls, domain-blocking tools, and network services can limit scripts, trackers, or third-party requests.

Blocking technologies at the browser or network level may override or operate independently from the preference stored by Echoform.

Do Not Track and Browser Privacy Signals

Some browsers or devices can send privacy signals such as “Do Not Track” or other opt-out preferences.

There is not always a single universally applied technical or legal standard for every signal. Echoform may respond to legally recognized browser signals where required and technically supported.

Your direct cookie-consent choice and browser-storage settings remain the clearest controls available through the standard website configuration.

What Happens When Storage Is Disabled

Disabling optional technologies should not prevent access to the website’s essential acoustic information.

Disabling or clearing all browser storage may cause:

  • The cookie notice to appear again on later visits.
  • Preference choices to reset.
  • Temporary form or interface state to be lost.
  • Some third-party visual resources or interactive features to load differently.
  • Security or anti-spam features to operate less effectively.
  • Website analytics to become less complete.

If a required website function does not work after blocking storage or scripts, you may need to allow essential site data or use another supported browser configuration.
