Different Goals

Treatment or Isolation

Both approaches address sound, but they solve different problems and require different decisions.

Inside the room

Acoustic Treatment

Treatment changes how sound behaves within a space by controlling reflections and reducing distracting room energy.

  • Controls early reflections
  • Reduces audible echo
  • Improves speech clarity
  • Changes sound inside the room
Explore treatment
Between spaces

Sound Isolation

Soundproofing aims to reduce transmission through construction, openings, connected surfaces, and flanking paths.

  • Depends on the building structure
  • Uses mass, sealing, and decoupling
  • Requires attention to gaps and routes
  • Cannot be achieved with foam alone
Understand isolation
Room Diagnosis

Hear the Problem

A room can feel visually complete while reflections, boundaries, and placement still work against speech, recording, or listening.

  • Flutter echo Repeated reflections between parallel hard surfaces.
  • Harsh reflections Strong nearby surfaces colouring speech or monitoring.
  • Weak speech clarity Room energy making voices feel distant or smeared.
  • Bass build-up Low frequencies accumulating around boundaries and corners.
  • Leakage expectations Confusing internal treatment with structural isolation.
